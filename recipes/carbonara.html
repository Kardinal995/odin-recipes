Carbonara Recipe

Description

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Roman dish that creates a rich, creamy sauce without cream by tossing hot pasta with fried guanciale (or pancetta/bacon), raw eggs/yolks, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper.

The heat from the pasta gently cooks the eggs into a glossy, velvety sauce.

The ingredients are simple—just spaghetti (or another long pasta), and the carbonara is made with pancetta or bacon, eggs, Parmesan, a little olive oil, salt and pepper. The silky carbonara sauce is created when the beaten eggs are tossed with the hot pasta and a little fat from the pancetta or bacon.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound pancetta or thick cut bacon, diced
  • 1 to 2 garlic cloves, minced, about 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • 3 to 4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or pecorino cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti (or bucatini or fettuccine)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
